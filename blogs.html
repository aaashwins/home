The Art of Staying Calm in a Chaotic World

In the world where there is a lot of notifications, likes, comments, and people who might trigger us to react. The ability to stay calm is not everyone’s cup of tea.
Calmness is a state of mental, emotional, and physical peace. It’s the ability to remain composed and balanced, especially in stressful or challenging situations. It doesn’t mean absence of problems or chaos; rather, it’s about how you respond to them.

I used to think being calm meant staying silent—speaking only when necessary, keeping my words brief, and never reacting too quickly. I would sit quietly in my friend group, watching conversations unfold around me, barely contributing. I convinced myself that this restraint was calmness, that I was composed and in control. Silence became my shield, a way to avoid conflicts and maintain an illusion of peace.

But one day, it hit me. If being silent is calm, then a mute person must live in perfect calmness their entire life. That thought stayed with me and started unraveling my idea of what it truly means to be calm. I realized calmness isn’t about how much or how little you speak. It’s not about silence at all.

Calmness is about how you feel inside. It’s the ability to speak with intention and listen with presence. It’s about not letting your emotions control your words, not reacting impulsively, but responding with clarity. You can be calm and still speak freely. You can be calm and still express yourself fully. Being calm is a state of mind, not a lack of sound. It’s the space between reaction and response, the breath you take before you speak, the steadiness you find in chaos.
